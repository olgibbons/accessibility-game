Riding Permit Terms & Conditions

Please review the conditions for operating a dinosaur in shared airspace and public grazing zones.

Under Schedule D, clause 7(a) of the B49a Mesozoic Animal Transit and Licensing Act (1993), all mounted land dinosaurs are classified as Class 6 road vehicles, regardless of size. As such, they must have a valid MOT and are subject to road tax. To comply with ULEZ regulations, dinosaur riders are required to submit quarterly flatus emissions readings.

Permit applicants are solely liable for tail swipes, spontaneous roars, and other involuntary expressions as defined by the Ministry of Big Beasts (MoBB). All riders must wear appropriate impact gear as defined in Schedule E, paragraph 3.

Flying dinosaurs are not permitted to enter British airspace without prior authorisation from the Civil Aerodactyl Authority. Please note: pterosaurs are not legally classified as dinosaurs under the B49a Act and must be registered under a separate application process (Form PTER/2B). Unauthorised winged reptiles may be subject to fines, confiscation, or relocation to designated Jurassic Holding Zones.

Clause 11(f) prohibits airborne dinosaur traversal during peak hours (07:00–10:00 and 16:00–19:00) unless given exception under the SkyRider Pilot Grant. Violation results in a level-4 pterosaurity fine. Permits are null if dino mood is rated below “neutral.”
